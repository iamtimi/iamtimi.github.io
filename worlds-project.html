My Favorite Players at Worlds

Ongoing Project (2016-present)


The Details

Platform: Tumblr
Audience: 20,000+ personal blog followers and other League of Legends esports fans
Images: LCK flickr, LoL Esports flickr, official team photos, player instagrams

Project Background

I began my unique esports fan journey back in 2015 with the Mid-Season Invitational. I knew nothing about the game then (other than it was quite popular), but I was immediately intrigued by how fun and lively the scene was that I started playing the game afterwards - still watching the pro games too of course!

As I continued watching the teams and the players and becoming more familiar with the esports fan community, I wanted a way to share a piece of this world with others. I wanted to create something that both current fans and newbies (or those are aren't familiar at all) could enjoy. I figured the best way to do that and showcase the scene was to display the most important tournament of the year: The League of Legends World Championship. So in 2016, I started a mini poster project called "My Favorite Players at Worlds". It's a small way for me to celebrate the players and teams I cheer for for making it to Worlds, share the happiness with fellow fans, and hopefully, bring in new fans as well.

Initially, I did not plan for this to turn into a long-term project, I just wanted to share my joy with others, but now every year I make sure to create something to highlight and introduce players across the different regions.

Concluding Thoughts

Before this specific project, I would make and post GIFs of players, share news and updates of the different teams, post official stream links and videos all as ways to share the esports community with my followers and fellow fans. I made sure the content was still enjoyable to those that don't know much or anything about esports and always answered any curious questions I got. However, it was through this project that I really was able to create something of my own. I also achieved what I had set out to do: connect with other fans and share part of how I esport with them. There's been many fun comments left on my posts throughout the series:



What started as a pure passion project has turned into a piece that grew with me, both as a fan and as a designer. Sometimes we are not aware of our own growth, but by accidentally documenting my design style and progress year by year, I was able to see mine. I noticed I became bolder and more comfortable with my process and understanding what type of information I want the images to relay.

I look forward to how I will grow next in the upcoming 2021's World Championships and what creation will come from it.
