My Favorite Players at Worlds

Ongoing Project (2016-present)


The Details

Platform: Tumblr
Audience: Over 20,000 personal blog followers + other fans on the platform who can view through tags
Images: LCK flickr, LoL Esports flickr, official team photos

Project Background

I began my unique esports fan journey back in 2015 with the Mid-Season Invitational. I actually knew nothing about the game then, but I was so intrigued by how fun and lively the scene was that I started playing the game afterwards - still watching the esport too of course!

As I continued learning the game, as I continued watching the teams and the players, I wanted a way to share a piece of this world with others. I figured the best way to showcase the scene was to showcase the most important tournament of the year: the World Championships. So in 2016, I started a mini poster project called "My Favorite Players at Worlds". It's a small way for me to celebrate the players and teams for making it to Worlds, share the happiness with fellow fans, and hopefully, bring in new fans as well.

Actually, I did not expect this to turn into a longterm project when I made my first post back then, but now every year I make sure to create something to highlight and introduce players across the different regions.

These years are so old that I no longer have the original files to them. I was still wondering how I wanted to make the posts and the designs (if they are even able to classify as such) are very crude. But these posts were the start of it all and still hold the same excitement I feel each time Worlds rolls around.


Concluding Thoughts

Before this project and while Worlds was not happening, I would make and post GIFs of players, share news and updates of many different teams, share official stream links and videos to share the esports community with my followers and fellow fans. But it was through this project that I really was able to create something of my own. I also achieved what I had set out to do: connect with other fans and share part of how I esport with them. There's also been many fun comments left on my posts throughout the series:



What started as a pure passion project has turned into a something that grew with me, both as a fan and as a designer. Sometimes we are not aware of our own growth, but by accidentally documenting my design style and progress year by year, I noticed I became bolder year after year. Now, I look forward to how I will grow next in the upcoming 2021's World Championships and what creation will come from it.
